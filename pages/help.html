Help & Avoiding Scams

Stay safe when buying or selling online. craigslist is built on local, person-to-person deals. If an offer sounds too good to be true, it probably is. Here are some straightforward rules to help you stay safe.

🔒 General Safety Tips

  • Deal locally, face-to-face — meet in a public place such as a café, bank, or police station lobby.
  • Never wire money — Revolut, PayPal transfers, Western Union, or any wire service. Scammers often insist on these methods.
  • Use cash for smaller items — for larger amounts consider a bank transfer only after meeting the seller and inspecting the item.
  • Don't share personal information — never give out your bank details, National Insurance number, or copies of ID.
  • Inspect before paying — test electronics, read all documents, and inspect vehicles before handing over any money.
  • Trust your instincts — if something feels off, walk away.

⚠️ Common Scams to Watch For

  • Fake payment notifications — a buyer sends a fake PayPal email claiming money is "on hold" until you ship. Always log into your account directly to verify.
  • Overpayment / cashier's cheque scam — a buyer sends a cheque for more than the agreed amount and asks you to wire back the difference. The cheque later bounces, and you lose both the item and the money.
  • Fake escrow sites — a scammer suggests using a third party "guarantee" or escrow site that they control. Legitimate escrow is rarely used for classifieds.
  • Rental scams — someone "abroad" asks for a deposit before you've viewed the property. Always view a property in person before paying anything.
  • Below-market listings — items priced far below market value to lure you into sending money upfront. If it seems too cheap, it's probably a scam.

📞 If You've Been Scammed

Stop all communication immediately. Contact your bank if you have shared financial details. Report the incident to the following:

  • Action Fraud (UK's national fraud reporting service)
  • Use the flag link on the suspicious craigslist posting to report it to us
  • Forward scam emails to spam@craigslist.org
Remember: craigslist does not get involved in transactions, does not offer buyer protection, and never guarantees deals. Keep everything local and in person.

🚨 Reporting & Safety Resources

If your personal information has been posted on craigslist without consent, use our online removal form to report it. Ask other users contacting you to flag inappropriate postings directly.

If you are receiving unwanted messages or suspect a scam, the following resources can help:

  • Email harassment guidance
  • Phone harassment guidance
  • Reporting scams to Action Fraud

Please contact local law enforcement if you feel threatened or unsafe.
